abstract: 'In this paper, we propose a novel vision-based 3D software sensor using high-frame-rate (HFR) videos for bridge measurement that can realize non-contact full-view 3D dynamic analysis, including displacement distribution and frequency response. Model-based 3D-to-2D projection is applied for addressing weak-texture bridge images to effciently match corresponding points in stereo HFR 4K videos. Then relative 3D displacement is precisely calculated based on a pair of 2D displacement vectors, which are estimated on each camera by digital image correlation with subpixel accuracy. 3D displacement signals are analyzed by short-time Fourier transform for vibration analysis to calculate frequency responses of all measured points, and 3D mode shapes of peak frequencies that refer to intrinsic structure characteristics are estimated in frequency domain. To verify its effectiveness, a prestressed concrete girder bridge of 23 m length in Japan was measured and the experimental results show that our software sensor can achieve multi-point 3D displacement measurement with an accuracy of 0.01 mm and 3D vibration analysis in the frequency range of 0–50 Hz.'
